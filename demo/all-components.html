What is Edge Delivery?

Adobe Edge Delivery Services is a composable, high-performance delivery platform. Content is authored in Google Docs or Microsoft SharePoint and served directly from 300+ global edge nodes with zero infrastructure management.

Why It Matters

Every 100 ms of page latency costs roughly 1% in conversions. EDS eliminates render-blocking resources and heavy client-side frameworks so your pages are fast by default, not by accident.

Teams ship 10× faster because developers and authors share a single source of truth — no exports, no sync jobs, no middleware between the document and the live page.

Traffic spikes are absorbed automatically. Capacity is infinite at the edge. Your ops team gets their weekends back because there are no servers to patch or scale.

Adobe Edge Delivery Services (EDS) is a modern web delivery platform that rethinks how enterprise content reaches end users. It strips away the layers of middleware, server-side rendering queues, and heavy client frameworks that make traditional CMS platforms slow. Instead, content is authored in documents you already know — Google Docs or Microsoft Word — and published as optimised HTML served instantly from the edge. The result is a consistent 100/100 Lighthouse score, sub-second Time to First Byte, and a development model that feels like building for the open web again.

The Cost of Slow Sites

Every 100 ms of latency costs approximately 1% in conversions. Traditional CMS platforms ship megabytes of JavaScript before a single pixel renders on screen, punishing mobile users and destroying search rankings.

The Edge Delivery Solution

By moving rendering to the edge and eliminating client-side frameworks from the critical path, EDS achieves sub-second Time to First Byte from anywhere on the planet — without a CDN warm-up period.
